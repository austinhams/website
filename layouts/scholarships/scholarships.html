Club Scholarships

The Austin Amateur Radio Club is proud to support the next generation of innovators, communicators, engineers, and community leaders through scholarship programs for students in Central Texas.

Amateur Radio develops practical skills that extend far beyond the radio itself — including engineering, problem solving, emergency communications, public service, leadership, and lifelong learning. Through these scholarships, AARC invests in students who are building the future of technology and community service.

Whether you’re pursuing engineering, computer science, public service, healthcare, skilled trades, or another field of study, AARC scholarships help students continue their education while recognizing achievement, service, and involvement in Amateur Radio. Students are encouraged to apply to all scholarships for which they are eligible.

Who can apply

Students pursuing higher education or skilled trades — engineering, computer science, public service, healthcare, and more.

Where you live

Open to residents of the Austin area: Travis, Bastrop, Blanco, Burnet, Caldwell, Hays, and Williamson counties.

When to apply

Applications open May 1 and close July 31 each year.

How to apply

Three simple steps to submit your application.

  1. 1

    Confirm eligibility

    Review the criteria above and gather the documents you’ll need (transcript, references, essay).

  2. 2

    Complete the application

    Submit your application online through our grants portal between May 1 and July 31.

  3. 3

    Hear back

    Recipients are notified in the late summer and recognized by the club community.

Ready to apply?

Applications are accepted each year from May 1 through July 31 via our grants portal.
